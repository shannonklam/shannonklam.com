Shannon's README

If you're curious about how I work, then you've stumbled onto the right page. This is my guide to all of my remote work-related practices, including my habits, preferences, and quirks.

My Hours

You can officially reach me on Slack (@shannon) from 7:30 to 16:30 PST, Monday through Friday.

When it comes to actually doing my work, though, it's a different story. I focus best in small chunks, so I tend to start my work early in the morning and not finish until late at night.

Here's a typical day for me:

  • 6:30: Wake up
  • 7:00: Work
  • 10:30: Walk the dogs
  • 11:30: Work
  • 13:00: Do chores/run errands
  • 17:30: Work
  • 19:00: Have dinner
  • 21:00: Work
  • 22:30: Go to bed

You'll also find me working on the weekends pretty regularly.

Why do I split up my work so much?

Because it gives me a chance to ruminate on what I've read or researched instead of running with my first impression. I find the quality of my work improves this way.

How to Contact Me

Slack is always the best way to get in touch with me. Please send me a Slack message at any time! I promise you won't be bothering me. If I'm not available, I'll just respond when I'm next online.

For video meetings, I usually use Google Hangouts or Zoom. Feel free to schedule a video chat with me anytime! I just ask for at least a day's heads-up to plan around our call. That way, I can make sure you'll have my full, undivided attention.

How I Communicate

I may be an introvert, but that doesn't mean I don't like chatting with my teammates! I love getting to know the people I'm working with.

I try to be friendly, honest, and open when communicating. My goal is to avoid time-wasting misunderstandings; to that end, I work hard to fully explain what I'm thinking (so forgive me if I get a little verbose at times!).

Here's what will help me communicate with you best:

  • Be specific. The more detailed, the better! I'd rather have too much to review than too little.
  • Provide a timeframe. It's okay if you don't have a firm due date in mind. A rough timeline should be enough to give me an idea of how to prioritize your ask.
  • Don't wait. The sooner we can get the ball rolling, the more time we'll have to iron things out. This goes doubly if there's an existing issue you're bringing to light. Don't sit on it! Let me know so I can get to fixing it right away.
  • Don't be afraid to bring it up! We're all professionals who value reasoned discussion and debate. I will always assume you mean well; you won't hurt my feelings.
